In this mode (this HTML page), you can only browse static JPEG images
converted from the original files with fixed window and zoom levels.

Viewing an image Just click on a thumbnail on the left part of this screen.
You can adjust the proportion between the left and right pane by moving the vertical line that splits the screen in two.
Browsing images inside a serie Use the and buttons, or the combo list.
Back to This screen... Just click
About the image size... When you click on images for the first time, they may be larger than the viewing pane. Click on the image again or on the icon
When an image is fitted to the pane, click on it to view it at its original size (may be larger than the screen).
Full screen mode If you are browsing this page using Microsoft Internet Explorer, press F11 on your keyboard to switch to full screen mode. Press F11 again to come back to the windowed mode.

If you need to work directly on the native DICOM images,
you must browse them with a specific DICOM viewer...

Step 1 A DICOM viewer must be installed on your machine. If not, click on this link to install the version of DicomWorks which is included with these images, or check our web site to get the latest version.
Step 2A If DicomWorks is installed on your machine, you can click on the small icon beside each study description to automatically launch it in DicomWorks.

Note 1 : If DicomWorks is NOT installed on your machine, you may get an error message when clicking on these links (see "Step 2B").
Note 2 : When you click on the study icon for the first time, you are prompted to "Open" or "Save to disk" the file. =>UNcheck the checkbox which enables the message box at each click on a DicomWorks link (dlk) and click on the button "Open" . This message box will no longer bother you...
Step 2B

If DicomWorks is NOT installed on your machine, first open a DICOM viewer and open the CD-ROM if the application can browse it (most can't).
